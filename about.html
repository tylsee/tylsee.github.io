A Festival of Discovery…

Inspired by the concept of farewell events like Britain's Reading Festival, Lollapalooza was conceived in 1990 by Perry Farrell, Ted Gardner, Don Muller, and Marc Geiger as a send-off for Farrell's band, Jane's Addiction.

Unlike previous festivals, Lollapalooza adopted a touring format across the United States and Canada from mid-July to late August 1991, featuring a diverse lineup spanning alternative rock, industrial music, and rap.

The inaugural tour kicked off in Phoenix, Arizona, covered by MTV, and concluded near Seattle, Washington. Central to Lollapalooza's appeal was its incorporation of non-musical elements, including alternative freak shows, art displays, and political awareness campaigns, reflecting Farrell's desire for unexpected and slightly bizarre experiences within rock culture.
